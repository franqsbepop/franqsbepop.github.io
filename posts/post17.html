There is always an ε: On the existence of God

Date: January 15, 2025

The question of the existence of God has been a topic of philosophical, theological, and scientific debate for centuries. While some argue that the existence of God can be proven through logical reasoning and empirical evidence, others believe that it is a matter of faith that lies beyond the realm of human understanding.

One of the most famous arguments for the existence of God is the Ontological Argument, which was first proposed by St. Anselm in the 11th century. This argument posits that the very concept of God as the greatest being implies His existence. If we can conceive of a being greater than which nothing can be conceived, then this being must exist in reality, because existence in reality is greater than existence in the mind alone[1].

Another well-known argument is the Cosmological Argument, which asserts that everything that exists has a cause, and since the universe exists, it must have a cause, which is God. This argument is rooted in the principle of causality and has been supported by various philosophers and theologians throughout history, including Thomas Aquinas and Leibniz[2].

The Teleological Argument, or the argument from design, suggests that the complexity and order in the universe point to the existence of an intelligent designer. This argument has been bolstered by discoveries in modern science, such as the fine-tuning of the physical constants and the intricate structures of biological organisms, which some argue are best explained by the existence of a purposeful creator[3].
