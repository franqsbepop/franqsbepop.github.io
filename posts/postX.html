Mathematical Modelling in Stochastic Analysis and Finance

Date: November 13, 2024

The proposed project aims to establish a comprehensive framework for mathematical modelling at the intersection of stochastic analysis and mathematical finance. The primary objective is to develop robust models that accurately capture the dynamics of financial markets and other stochastic systems. This involves integrating advanced mathematical techniques with data-driven approaches to enhance the predictive power and reliability of the models.

Firstly, we will explore the theoretical underpinnings of stochastic processes and their applications in financial modelling. This includes a detailed examination of stochastic differential equations (SDEs) and their role in describing the evolution of asset prices and other financial variables[1]. We will also investigate the use of partial differential equations (PDEs) in option pricing and risk management[2].

Secondly, the project will focus on the integration of machine learning techniques with traditional stochastic models. This includes the application of neural networks, reinforcement learning, and other AI methodologies to improve model accuracy and adaptability[3]. We will also explore the use of generative models for simulating financial time series and other stochastic processes[4].

Thirdly, we will apply these advanced modelling techniques to real-world financial problems, such as portfolio optimization, risk assessment, and algorithmic trading. By leveraging the power of stochastic analysis and machine learning, we aim to develop innovative solutions that can adapt to the ever-changing landscape of financial markets[5].

Finally, the project will include a rigorous validation phase, where the developed models will be tested against historical data and benchmarked against existing models. This will ensure that the proposed methodologies are not only theoretically sound but also practically viable[6].
