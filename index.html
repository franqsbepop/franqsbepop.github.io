Biography

I am a Mathematics Ph.D. candidate at Instituto Superior Técnico, Universidade de Lisboa (IST-UL), supported by a doctoral scholarship from the FCT (Foundation for Science and Technology). I work on the interface of stochastic analysis and optimal transport with applications to finance and learning dynamics. My research is carried out at the Grupo de Física Matemática (GFM) and the Korteweg-de Vries Institute for Mathematics (KdVI), under the co-supervision of Sven Karbach and Léonard Monsaingeon. I hold an MSc in Stochastics and Financial Mathematics from the University of Amsterdam, where I was supervised by Asma Khedher, and a BSc in Mathematics from Lancaster University, where my academic advisor was Yemon Choi. I am passionate about fostering innovation in technology and science. I run Gradient that organizes hackathons such as Junction in Portugal and in the organization of the Portuguese Olympiads in Artificial Intelligence.
